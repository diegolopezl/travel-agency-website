About Nara

奈良について

Nara is a city located in the Nara Prefecture of Japan. It is a historic city and was the capital of Japan from 710 to 784. Nara is known for its well-preserved temples, shrines, and ruins, as well as its abundance of free-ranging deer that roam the city. Some of the must-visit sights in Nara include Todai-ji Temple, Kasuga-taisha Shrine, and the Nara Park, where visitors can interact with the city's famous deer. In addition to its historical and cultural attractions, Nara also offers a number of natural scenic spots such as Mount Wakakusa and the Yoshino and Omine mountain ranges.
